    ABOUT

    Solar panels, also known as photovoltaic (PV) modules, are innovative devices designed to harness sunlight and convert it into electricity. Comprising multiple solar cells made of semiconductor materials like silicon, these panels utilize the photovoltaic effect to generate electrical current when exposed to sunlight. Each solar cell consists of layers with differing electrical charges, creating an electric field that allows the flow of electricity. Solar panels play a crucial role in the renewable energy landscape, providing a sustainable and environmentally friendly alternative to traditional energy sources. They contribute significantly to reducing carbon emissions and dependence on fossil fuels. The energy produced by solar panels can be used for various applications, from powering residential homes and commercial buildings to supporting off-grid installations in remote areas.The efficiency of solar panels is continually improving, with advancements in technology enhancing their performance and affordability. Different types of solar panels, such as monocrystalline, polycrystalline, and thin-film, offer varying levels of efficiency and cost-effectiveness, allowing consumers to choose based on their specific needs and budget.
